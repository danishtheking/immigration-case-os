## Petition Letter Structure

### O-1A / EB-1A Petition Format

1. **Introduction** — Identify petitioner, beneficiary, classification sought
2. **Statutory Framework** — Cite INA section, 8 CFR regulatory criteria
3. **Beneficiary's Qualifications** — Summary of extraordinary ability
4. **Evidence by Criterion** (must meet 3 of 8):
   - Awards/prizes of national or international recognition
   - Membership in associations requiring outstanding achievement
   - Published material about the beneficiary
   - Judging the work of others
   - Original contributions of major significance
   - Scholarly articles in professional journals
   - Employment in a critical or essential capacity
   - High salary or remuneration
5. **Final Merits Determination** (Kazarian two-step analysis)
   - Step 1: Evidence meets at least 3 criteria
   - Step 2: Totality of evidence demonstrates sustained acclaim
6. **Conclusion** — Request for approval

### EB-2 NIW Petition Format (Matter of Dhanasar)

1. **Introduction**
2. **The Proposed Endeavor** — What the beneficiary intends to do
3. **Prong 1: Substantial Merit and National Importance**
   - Scope of the endeavor
   - Impact on the field/nation
4. **Prong 2: Well-Positioned to Advance the Endeavor**
   - Education, skills, knowledge
   - Track record of success
   - Progress toward the endeavor
   - Interest from relevant parties
5. **Prong 3: Balance of Equities — Waiver Benefits the U.S.**
   - Urgency of the beneficiary's contributions
   - Impracticality of labor certification
   - National benefit outweighs labor market protections
6. **Conclusion**

## Writing Guidelines

- Use formal legal writing style
- Cite relevant case law (Kazarian v. USCIS, Matter of Dhanasar, etc.)
- Reference specific evidence exhibits (Exhibit A, B, C...)
- Include page references to supporting documents
- Use persuasive but factual language — avoid hyperbole
- Address potential weaknesses proactively
- Follow the AAO's analytical framework

## Key Case Law References

- **Kazarian v. USCIS, 596 F.3d 1115 (9th Cir. 2010)** — Two-step analysis for EB-1A/O-1
- **Matter of Dhanasar, 26 I&N Dec. 884 (AAO 2016)** — NIW three-prong framework
- **NYSDOT, 22 I&N Dec. 215 (AAO 1998)** — Prior NIW framework (superseded by Dhanasar)
- **Buletini v. INS, 860 F. Supp. 1222 (E.D. Mich. 1994)** — Comparable evidence
- **Matter of Caron International, 19 I&N Dec. 791 (BIA 1988)** — Speciality occupation
